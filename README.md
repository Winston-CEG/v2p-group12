# SafeCross V2P

**A C-V2X Haptic Alert System for Visually Impaired Pedestrians**

CEG3006 Group Project | AY2025/26 Tri 2 | Group 12

---

## Project Description

SafeCross V2P is a Vehicle-to-Pedestrian safety system that uses C-V2X PC5 sidelink direct communication to deliver real-time haptic warnings to visually impaired (VI) pedestrians via a wearable accessory. Unlike existing V2P prototypes that rely on phone screens or audio alerts, SafeCross provides non-visual, hands-free situational awareness by receiving cooperative vehicle broadcasts directly — without depending on the pedestrian's own sensors or eyesight.

Approximately 3–4% of Singaporean adults aged 40 and above live with some form of visual impairment (Singapore Epidemiology of Eye Disease Study). For these individuals, crossing roads — particularly at complex intersections — poses a serious safety risk. SafeCross aims to bridge the gap between vehicle-side cooperative awareness and pedestrian-side assistive technology.

## Target Audience

- **Primary:** Pedestrians who are blind or have low vision, particularly those who already use mobility aids such as white canes
- **Secondary:** Elderly pedestrians with age-related vision deterioration (e.g., cataracts, glaucoma, macular degeneration)
- **Tertiary:** Caregivers and mobility specialists who support VI individuals in urban navigation
